Nidhi Rules

Nidhi company rules require a public company with a minimum paid-up equity share capital of ₹10 lakh and the suffix "Nidhi Limited" in its name. Within one year, it must have at least 200 members, a Net Owned Funds (NOF) of ₹20 lakh or more, and unencumbered term deposits equal to at least 10% of its outstanding deposits. Key regulations include accepting deposits and lending only to members, not issuing preference shares, and adhering to specific loan-to-value ratios for loans secured against gold or property.

Membership and capital

  • Must be a public company with "Nidhi Limited" as the last part of its name.
  • Minimum paid-up equity share capital of ₹10 lakh.
  • Minimum of 200 members within one year of incorporation.
  • Must have a Net Owned Funds (NOF) of ₹20 lakh or more within one year.
  • A minimum of 3 directors and 7 members are required at the time of incorporation.

Operations and Deposits

  • Accept deposits and provide loans exclusively to its members.
  • Cannot accept deposits from or lend to persons other than its members.
  • Cannot issue preference shares; existing preference shares must be redeemed according to their terms.
  • Must maintain unencumbered term deposits of at least 10% of its outstanding deposits.
  • The ratio of Net Owned Funds to deposits must not exceed 1:20.
  • Cannot pledge assets lodged by members as security.
  • Cannot have any business objective other than cultivating savings and thrift among members.
  • May provide locker facilities to members, but the rental income cannot exceed 20% of the company's gross income in a financial year.

Governance and compliance

  • The company must comply with all applicable rules and file annual returns with the Ministry of Corporate Affairs.
  • Directors must be members of the company; no outside directors are allowed.
  • Must hold regular board meetings and maintain proper books of accounts.
  • The company cannot declare a dividend exceeding 25% in a financial year.
  • Cannot issue any advertisement for soliciting deposits, except for private circulation to members only.

Loans and Interest

  • The rate of interest on loans cannot exceed 7.5% above the highest rate offered on deposits by the Nidhi.
  • Interest rates on all classes of loans must be prominently displayed at the registered office and all branch offices.
  • For gold or jewelry-backed loans, the loan-to-value ratio cannot exceed 80%.
